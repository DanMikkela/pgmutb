Burmilla - the Breed

The story behind the Burmilla

The breed has its origin in England, and was there the first matings were done and litters born. The kittens were extremely enchanting, a new breed was born. In 1984 there was an export of a Burmilla to Denmark, Penric Quicksilver. From this point of time, the Burmillas has grown steadily in numbers and popularity with the public. In 1994 an initiativewas taken to have the breed officially accepted for showing within FIFé. It has been accepted since 1/1-95.

The personality

The temperament is gentle, sweet and exstreamly friendly. It's very extravert and social, it simply LOVES people - adults and children. They are great "talkers", leaving comments and remarks on most things You do around the house. You won't be left unaware about the fact that there is a Burmilla in the house! An enchanting and adorable expression completes the Burmilla charm.

The Burmilla Standard

The Burmilla is an elegant cat of medium foreign type in Shaded and Tipped (Shell) Silver coat varieties. The standard is described on Standard page.

Burmilla colours and official code

The official code of the Burmilla is BML (EMS-code).

One of the fascinating qualities of the Burmilla is the shimmer of the coat. This is the result of the colour disposition on each hair, white base colour (Silver) and tipped at the top end with a darker colour. The amount of tipping is denoted by Shell (12) for maximum ¼ of colour and Shaded (11) giving ¼ - ½ of colour. Since colour code n (neutral) denotes both black (Persian heritage) and brown (Burmese heritage), an additional code 31 is appended when Burmese brown. (not officially yet, but ...)
