American Lynx / American Bobtail CCA Standards

shorthair and semi-longhair, body type: cobby (Abbreviation: ALX)


GENERAL: This breed (also called American Bobtail) was developed in North Carolina and Seattle in the 1980s. Although it looks like a lynx it is just a mix of different breeds, not a cross-breed with a real lynx.

BODY: big, muscular, heavy boning.

HEAD: broad wedge shape, slight pinch. Skull medium to big. High, strong cheekbones. Strong chin, broad muzzle.

NOSE: broad, medium length.

EARS: big, set far apart, wide at base. The ears have "lynx tips".

EYES: almond shaped, big, set on slight angle.

EYE COLOR: green, orange, amber, gold, hazel or copper. Green, gold or hazel preferred.

LEGS: long, strong. Hind legs a bit longer than those in the front.

FEET: big, round. Toes: five in front and four behind.

TAIL: short. Should not be longer than the knees of the hindlegs. Touching it must not be painful for the cat!
Tail coat:
SHORTHAIR: short and dense.
LONGHAIR: full and plumelike.

COAT:
SHORTHAIR: short, dense, glossy.
LONGHAIR: semi-long, laying flat, ruff around the neck.

PENALIZE: ears low set; small ears; strong pinch; body too cobby; slim body; excessive size; deep nose break; white patches; tail too short. Incorrect color of nose leather or paw pads.

DISQUALIFY: Tail faults; squinting; incorrect number of toes; touching the tail is painful for the cat.

COLORS:

LEOPARD SPOTTED: see spotted tabby. Colors: black, blue, chocolate, lilac, cinnamon, fawn. All these colors with or without silver. No reds, creams or torties.

TAWNY: see ticked tabby. Colors: black, blue, chocolate, lilac, cinnamon, fawn. All these colors with or without silver. No reds, creams or torties.

ALLOWABLE OUTCROSS BREEDS:
none
